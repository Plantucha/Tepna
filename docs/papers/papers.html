Tepna Working Preprints

Findings from the physiological-signal suite, written up so they need not be rediscovered. Each paper regenerates its tables and figures deterministically from a named, local analysis tool: open the tool, set N, and Run. Synthetic cohorts are seeded (CohortGen.patient(k) per index; synth-gen default seed 424242), so a rerun reproduces the numbers within sampling error at the stated N — on the current generator synth-gen 2.1 / cohort-gen 1.9. Standardized format: paper.css. Drafts — not peer-reviewed, not for clinical use.

What changed in the July 2026 re-texture rerun (synth-gen 2.1 / cohort-gen 1.9). Re-running the six simulation papers on a more realistic broadband RR texture flipped two headline theses: optical rMSSD is now unbiased-but-noisier rather than “a different quantity” (rmssd-equivalence), and autonomic rMSSD now localizes the CPAP-start night better than respiratory ODI-4 (treatment-response). Read together, rmssd-equivalence (bias-inert optical error) and qrs-yield (yield-driven optical error) now tell one story: the optical arm's disagreement is a variance/yield penalty, not a bias.   • Draft v1 · methods / single-subject case study One phone is not one clock: inter-device timing drift defeats beat-level fusion of two consumer wearables

    A chest ECG (Polar H10) and an ankle raw-PPG (Verity Sense) logged by one phone are the same heartbeats (89% beat coupling) yet their R→foot lag drifts ~1.1 s/night at a fixed 47.7 ppm (37.7–55.3 across 11 nights, 145k beats) — ordinary crystal tolerance. Same-phone logging does not synchronize them: the phone timestamp equals the device sensor-clock to within ms over 6.6 h, so each stream rides its own crystal. Two passive accelerometer re-sync schemes (windowed + event-matched) fail — chest↔ankle motion is decorrelated. Conclusion: beat-level fusion needs a single acquisition clock (host-side SDK capture), not app-timestamped dual-BLE logs. Standalone account of dead-ends wall 2.7.

  • Draft v2 · real-data method study Optical HRV against chest ECG on twenty overnight recordings: rMSSD error is fiducial jitter, and the jitter budget follows in closed form

    v2 supersedes the four-night v1 and revises its headline downward. 20 paired H10 + Verity Sense overnights, 1,477 five-minute epochs, 121.5 h, one subject. Beat detection is at ceiling (sensitivity and PPV both median 1.0000); what the optical arm loses is timing — jitter 5.92 ms, bias +0.01 ms. rMSSD runs +4.24% high (18/20 nights within ±10%), not the −0.6% v1 reported on its two best-of-four nights. The central result is mechanistic: rMSSD²ppg = rMSSD²ecg + kσ² fits at per-night R² = 0.964, k = 2.39 — so accuracy collapses to one scalar and the design budget inverts in closed form (σ ≤ 3.51 ms → 1% bias). Catastrophic epochs (10.6%) are just high-jitter epochs (35.0 vs 5.5 ms). Also documents why a single global affine clock map fails (PTT of −1 ms, F1 0.26) and records two negative results: waveform fusion of the 3 LEDs loses to best-single-channel, and the literature's preferred middle-amplitude fiducial is worse here than the shipped tangent.

  • Draft v1 · methods / negative result Pooling epochs inverts signal-quality gate rankings: a wearable-HRV cautionary result, and the vindication of a gate it condemned

    Choosing a signal-quality gate by pooling every epoch from every night is the obvious experiment, and on this corpus it gives the wrong answer decisively. Pooled over 1,488 ECG-referenced epochs, beat coverage wins (AUC 0.737, p=7×10⁻²³) and the shipped per-beat SQI looks like a coin flip (0.556). Clustered within nights — the analysis matching how a gate is actually deployed — the ranking exactly reverses: shipped SQI best (0.763, p=0.014), coverage worst and non-significant (0.629, p=0.106). The mechanism is an ecological fallacy: coverage varies between nights and tracks which nights are bad, so pooling lets between-night variation masquerade as within-night discrimination. Used as a real gate, SQI cuts p90 error 25.8%→21.4% at 70% retention while coverage makes the median worse (4.43%→4.89%). Acting on the pooled result would have deleted a working feature and replaced it with a harmful one. Honest coda: no gate here is transformative — error is governed by jitter, which none of them measures.

  • Draft v1 · real-data methods · tool not yet ported The machine in the bedroom: CPAP flow as a home reference standard for validating wearable respiratory algorithms

    Validating a wearable respiratory algorithm normally needs a sleep lab. But a ResMed CPAP already writes a calibrated 25 Hz flow signal to its SD card every night, plus AASM-typed scored events. This is the recipe for promoting that to a working reference: breath detection from the flow trace; validating the reference before scoring anything against it (two independent flow-derived estimators agree to MAE 0.70 brpm — the floor no algorithm can honestly beat); and recovering the clock relationship, which is neither known nor stable. The wearable and CPAP clocks differed by ≈39 minutes, recovered by cross-correlation to a ±4 s peak, and that offset drifts +0.589 s/day (residual SD 1.03 s over 37 days). Key practical finding: drift-consistency is a better validity test than correlation magnitude — a correlation gate would have discarded four good nights, non-randomly biased toward the hardest ones.

  • Draft v1 · real-data n-of-1 · tool not yet ported Overnight respiratory rate from a Polar H10 chest accelerometer during CPAP therapy

    The H10 streams a tri-axial accelerometer alongside its ECG, and it has apparently never been evaluated for respiration — the H10 literature is entirely HR/HRV. Nor has any published chest-accelerometer respiration study recorded treated nights. On 26 nights / 19,193 epochs against CPAP flow: MAE 1.01 brpm (95% CI 0.91–1.12), 91.6% within 2 brpm; on event-free epochs 0.77. Confidence-gated it reaches MAE 0.56 at 70% coverage (97.8% within 2 brpm) — the reference's own noise floor. Spectral ridge tracking by Viterbi across epochs, plus a time-domain blend (Charlton's 314-algorithm result put all top performers in the time domain). The shipped predecessor scores 3.59 — worse than predicting a constant. Runs in 0.17 s/night in dependency-free ES5. An explicit tilt-angle channel is measurably redundant (spectrum correlation +1.000). Dominant limitation is exposure, not accuracy: one subject, and — measured — essentially one posture.

  • Draft v1 · negative result · PARKED — see §5 Absent effort is not absent motion: chest-accelerometer effort fails to type apneas during CPAP therapy

    Central-vs-obstructive typing rests on effort being absent in central events. On 22 nights (370 central, 31 obstructive), accelerometer effort during central apneas sits at 0.99× its own night baseline — not absent, normal. Only 16.5% fall below half baseline, where a single RIP belt achieves 84% recall. Best discrimination is AUC 0.691 (p=0.0002) — real but far below useful. A methodological finding of independent interest: including each event's termination destroys the effect entirely (AUC 0.573, p=0.089), because the terminating arousal injects motion exactly where effort should look absent. Advanced as a hypothesis, not a conclusion: positive airway pressure may drive chest motion without respiratory effort — every prior study recorded untreated nights. Parked pending that test, obstructive n=31, device-scored labels, and an unresolved contrary report.

  • Draft v1 · real-data pilot Measuring a device's σ without a canonical reference: heart-rate error across O2Ring, Polar H10 and Verity Sense

    A three-rung recipe for stating a sensor's uncertainty when you own no calibrated reference: repeatability (no reference), transfer-standard Bland–Altman/Arms (promote the H10 ECG strap to ≈truth), and the three-cornered hat (three devices, none assumed canonical). On 291,561 co-recorded 1-Hz seconds (twenty-six nights) the O2Ring pulse is unbiased vs ECG (−0.20 bpm) but carries a random σ ≈ 3.2 bpm at rest (one motion night hit 9.9). The Verity Sense's onboard HR was dead — but its HR recovered cleanly from raw PPG (SQI ≈1.0), and with the H10 leg re-derived from raw ECG, the tool folder-ingests each night and solves a fused-weight artifact-robust hat across the full twenty-six-night corpus → 2.41 / 1.28 / 1.42 bpm (O2Ring / H10 / Verity). Per-second beat-confidence weighting down-weights transient artifacts in place (a spurious-QRS burst on 06-12 that detonates the plain hat to σH10 ≈9.6 is kept and cleaned to ≈2.6). The raw-ECG H10 is the quietest corner (the gold leg), the O2Ring pulse the noisiest — an earlier 6-window hand-picked hat had instead put Verity noisiest (3.50), a small-sample artefact the full corpus corrects. SpO₂ trueness stays out of reach (no arterial reference).

  • Draft v1 · sim + real validation Pinning a sensor's error without a reference: how many co-recorded windows, and how long each?

    The metrology twin of nights-icc: how many co-recorded O2Ring + H10 + Verity windows pin each device's reference-free error σ? Monte-Carlo (720 trials/cell, Web-Worker pool) over the same three-cornered-hat kernel with planted σ 2.7 / 1.9 / 1.9 bpm shows one ~1-hour window pins the whole trio to ±0.5 bpm; ±0.25 needs ~5–8, ±0.15 ~12–20. The hat couples the corners, so the noisiest corner (the O2Ring on this corpus) sets a shared floor (not "the noisy device needs far more data"), while the quietest corner is hardest to pin tight. The regime cost is bias, not N: a resting night under-states the instantaneous devices because the TCH strips shared HRV — a dynamic session recovers the full σ. Validated on a real 26-night tri-device corpus (folder-ingested, raw-ECG gold leg, fused-weight artifact-robust hat; O2Ring σ̂ ≈ 2.41, H10 ≈ 1.28, Verity ≈ 1.42 bpm; the quiet-order regime + motion premise confirmed) — modestly below the planted σ (kept at 2.7 / 1.9 / 1.9, the pre-fused seed), with the O2Ring the noisiest corner (an earlier hand-picked hat had put Verity noisiest, a small-sample artefact).
