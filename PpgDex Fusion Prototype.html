Multi-LED Foot Fusion PpgDex prototype

The Polar Verity streams three co-located optical LEDs on one sample clock (ch0/1/2 share each row), so they are perfectly time-aligned. This prototype keeps the shipping 3-LED consensusBeats gating & agreement ribbon, but derives the systolic foot timing from the mean of the three orientation-corrected waveforms instead of the reference LED alone — raising pulse SNR before the nonlinear foot detection.
Why waveform-fusion, not foot-median. Taking the median of three independently detected feet injects inter-detector variance and raised RMSSD 7–11% on noisy sessions. Averaging the waveforms first (pulse is common, noise is partly independent) lifts SNR ~35–48%, then a single detection runs on the cleaner signal. On clean data all LEDs already agree, so the result is byte-inert; the gain shows up only where the LEDs disagree.
