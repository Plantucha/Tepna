## ¬ß2 ‚Äî Recorded decision (do not re-litigate): scrub, not drop, `inputs[].name`

¬ß2 chose to **scrub** `inputs[].name` (keep vendor+lane+ext) rather than drop or hash it ‚Äî matching the
parent's treatment of `provenance.files` (one algorithm, `scrubFilename`, mirrored into
`ganglior-provenance.js`). The diagnostic value ("this came from a Polar `_RR` file") survives; the PHI does
not. `bytes`/`lastModifiedMs`/`sha256` were already identity-free and untouched. The committed PulseDex
fixtures were unaffected (headless `compute()` leaves provenance `null`); the scrub bites only REAL app
exports, which the gates don't capture ‚Äî so verify by inspecting a real app export's
`schema.provenance.inputs[]`, not via a fixture. No further action.

**Recorded this pass (2026-06-29 ‚Äî the agent surfaced these; the operator chose, directing the "all 7" execution):**
- **(a) ‚Üí NO subject key now; ¬ß3 stays UNBUILT/OFF.** The entire identity arc REMOVED durable identifiers
  (content-addressed digest, PHI-scrubbed filenames, no name/serial) ‚Äî a persisted subject key reintroduces

  strippable random-UUID design (never derived from name/DOB/serial), strip-on-share path designed first.
- **(b) ‚Üí posture DOCUMENTED, certification OPEN.** `PHI-SURFACE-STATEMENT.md` (new, this pass) is the
