PpgDex vs ECG — Ground-Truth Validation

The deciding test for the fusion work: one 7-hour overnight recorded simultaneously by two devices on the same body. The Polar H10 chest ECG (raw Pan–Tompkins) is the reference; the Verity optical PPG is the estimate under test. Same night, same beats — a true head-to-head.

Verdict

Optical PPG matches ECG on beat-to-beat HRV to within 2% — RMSSD 37.1 vs 37.8 ms, SD1 within 1.9%, and beat counts agree to 10 beats in 20,671 (0.05%). The pipeline is validated for the metrics fusion targets. As predicted, on this clean night (100% 3/3 LED agreement) fusion would be byte-inert — it neither needs to nor would disturb this agreement. Long-term/frequency metrics (SDNN, SD2, VLF power) run 26–89% high — a known optical pulse-transit-time artifact that fusion does not address.

Metric-by-metric agreement

Short-term HRV is validated. RMSSD, SD1, lnRMSSD, pNN50, HR and beat count all agree with ECG to ≤2%. These are the beat-to-beat metrics; adjacent-beat optical arrival jitter is small, so they survive the optical path intact. This is the strongest evidence yet that PpgDex's HRV is trustworthy on clean data — and that the fusion baseline is well-calibrated, sitting on the truth rather than over-smoothed below it.
Long-term / frequency HRV was inflated — SDNN now fixed, frequency flagged. As-measured: SDNN +26%, SD2 +54%, total power +89%, VLF +62%. The textbook PPG signature — respiration- and blood-pressure-driven pulse-transit-time modulation plus baseline wander adds low-frequency variance ECG doesn't see. SDNN is now corrected to +3.5% via segment-wise robust aggregation (shipped, below). The remaining frequency-domain inflation is genuinely PTT-contaminated and is flagged lower-confidence rather than "corrected" — and it is not something waveform fusion addresses either.

Three-way, same night: adding OxyDex

The O2Ring finger oximeter recorded the same 2026-07-07 night — a third modality (finger optical + SpO₂) alongside chest-electrical and wrist-optical. It samples HR at ~1 Hz, so it cannot produce beat-to-beat HRV (its “RMSSD” is a 1 Hz proxy and is excluded from that column) — but it is a powerful independent check on rate, and it alone carries oxygenation.
Three independent sensors agree on rate to under 1 bpm. Chest ECG 49.1, wrist PPG 50.0, finger oximeter 49.2 bpm mean HR on the same night — electrical, wrist-optical and finger-optical modalities converging validates that the rate backbone is real, not a per-device artifact. OxyDex also confirms the night was physiologically clean (SpO₂ mean 95.6%, min 88%, 0.1% of time <90%), so no desaturation/arousal load is confounding the HRV agreement above.

Making SDNN & the long-term metrics correct SHIPPED

Tested on the paired night: the long-term inflation is not within-beat error — it is between-segment drift (SDANN) plus a few motion/artifact epochs. It collapses with aggregation changes alone, no new sensing. Now implemented in ppgdex-dsp.js + ppgdex-app.js as additive fields sdnnIndex, sdnnRobust, sd2Robust, and lf/hf/lfhfRobust (whole-record values unchanged for back-compat); the app UI + node export surface the robust values, and the ladder below shows each metric family before→after.
One mechanism, three metric families corrected:
  • SDNN — whole-record +26% → sdnnRobust +3.5%. Gated median of per-5-min SDNN: reporting per-segment drops the between-segment SDANN drift, the median rejects a handful of high-motion epochs, and the quality gate (motionIndex/ledAgreementPct) removes the rest.
  • SD2 — whole-record +54% → sd2Robust +4.1%. SD2 is derived from SDNN, so rebuilding it from the robust dispersion + the (already-clean) beat-to-beat SD1 collapses the same inflation.
  • Band power — whole totalPower +89% → LF+HF robust median +6.9%. Per-epoch spectra can't see the slow between-segment drift, so aggregating them removes it; per-epoch LF agrees to +0.7%.
The two that stay honest — now graded, not blanket-flagged: instead of one binary lowConfidence, the export now carries a 0–1 confidence per metric family (hrv.confidence), each scored by the cause that predicts its error — validated on this night: HF excess is motion-driven (+43% high-motion vs +12% low), so HF confidence tracks the low-motion fraction and a stricter hfRobustLowMotion is emitted; VLF excess is baseline/posture drift (near-zero motion correlation), so VLF confidence tracks posture-stability and record length and is capped at 0.7 — single-site optical VLF stays baseline-wander-limited even when clean, which is a real property, not a defect to “fix.” A consumer can now down-weight proportionally instead of discarding.

What this does — and doesn't — settle for fusion

  • Settles: the PpgDex HRV baseline is accurate on clean nights, so fusion's clean-data inertness is a feature, not a limitation — it protects a result that's already correct.
  • Settles: even on this clean night PPG corrected 5.8% of beats vs ECG's 0.25% (23×). That correction headroom is exactly what fusion's SNR lift targets — fewer beats needing correction.
  • Still open: the decisive fusion test is a noisy paired night — does fusion pull a degraded PPG RMSSD toward the ECG truth? This clean night can't show that (there's no gap to close). Need an ECG+PPG pair from a high-motion / low-agreement night.
