Synthetic ground truth — read before citing. All three detectors score the same co-generated beats of one ~9-min window. PulseDex reads the ground-truth RR (artifact-cleaned) and is the reference; ECGDex re-derives beats from the raw int16 ECG (130 Hz, R-peaks rendered at their true beat times → faithful timing) via real Pan–Tompkins; PpgDex from the 176 Hz optical pulse. ECG−Pulse isolates 130 Hz sampling quantization + QRS-detection jitter; PPG−Pulse adds pulse-arrival-time jitter (and the residual optical over-detection); the quadrature excess of the PPG band over the ECG band is the PAT-jitter dispersion. This certifies the three nodes agree on rMSSD on shared beats and quantifies each modality's intrinsic dispersion — it is not a real-patient equivalence study, which needs simultaneous chest-ECG + finger/wrist-PPG recordings.
