PpgDex Fidelity Investigation

Process & findings · optical HRV pipeline · device VERITY-01 · drafted from real tri-device corpus

TL;DR

Of five candidate fidelity levers, only multi-LED waveform fusion survives as a safe, consistent win: +35–48% pulse SNR, RMSSD neutral-to-better on real sleep data, byte-inert on clean data. The other four were prototyped and three actively backfired — naive foot-median, ambient regression, and adaptive motion-gating all degraded the signal on real recordings; robust reference selection was a measured no-op. Recommendation: adopt waveform fusion alone; do not stack the others. One gate remains before shipping: validate fusion's lower RMSSD against the contemporaneous Polar H10 ECG ground truth.

1 · Motivation

The question began as "should PpgDex check SNR every window and use the best LED per window?" Investigating it against real data opened the broader question: where is there genuine fidelity to gain in the optical HRV pipeline? PpgDex streams three co-located optical LEDs (ch0/1/2) on one sample clock, runs a 3-LED consensus detector, and derives foot-to-foot HRV. Five levers were proposed and each was prototyped on real captures rather than argued in the abstract.

2 · Corpus & method

Real Polar Verity Sense captures (device VERITY-01), ~9 sessions incl. two full overnights (0616: 72×90 s windows; 0706: 17 parts, ~6.75 h, 262 windows), fs ≈ 176 Hz, plus paired ACC/GYRO for the motion-gate test. Large files were sliced with the project's ECG Splitter into ≤19 MB header-preserving parts so the real PPGDSP modules could run on them unmodified. Every prototype reuses the shipping functions (detectChannel, consensusBeats, refineFeet, correctRR, timeDomain); the baseline is always the current production output. Metrics: pulse-band SNR (0.7–3 Hz ÷ 4–8 Hz power), RMSSD, beats kept, and correction rate. Clean vs noisy sessions are separated because every lever behaves differently under disagreement.

Cross-night baseline characterization

  • Best LED is session-dependent — ch0, ch1 and ch2 each win on some session; even two sessions of the same night differ. A hardcoded reference would be wrong for a real fraction of recordings, so the per-recording pick is necessary.
  • Within a night the winner is stable, with drift confined to start/end transitions (settling-in, waking). Margins are usually <2.5% but reach ~9% on some sessions (0612b) — where large, they are stable, so the per-recording pick already captures them.
  • Per-window LED switching is not warranted — every within-night switch observed was a sub-1% near-tie flip or a transition the mid-window pick already handles.
  • Consensus 3/3 agreement ranges 50–77% on motion/settling clips up to 99.6% on clean overnights, with 1/3 drops tracking quality exactly — validating the consensus detector.

3 · The five levers — prototype by prototype

① Multi-LED waveform fusion winner

Hypothesis
Combine the three co-located, sample-aligned LEDs to raise pulse SNR before the nonlinear foot detection, tightening HRV and sharpening morphology.
First try
Falsified: taking the median of three independently-detected feet injects inter-detector variance and raised RMSSD 7–11% on noisy sessions (0611a +7.2%, 0614a +10.6%). Averaging detected positions gets no √N benefit.
Correct form
Average the three orientation-corrected waveforms, then detect once on the cleaner signal; keep consensusBeats for gating + the agreement ribbon.
Result
SNR +35–48% every session · RMSSD: 0706 clean 39.2→39.2 (inert), 0614a 65.1→61.1, 0621 48.2→47.6 — same beats, same correction (jitter removed, not variability). Caveat: 0611a, a 1.9 min settling clip, rose +18.9 ms (already flagged low-confidence).
Verdict
Adopt. The only lever that consistently helps and never harms real sleep data. Reuse the fused signal as the morphology/PI reference for the SNR lift.

④ Robust reference selection no-op

Hypothesis
Scoring the reference LED on a single mid-window can mis-rank; use a median rank over several windows plus a perfusion tie-break.
Result
Median-rank picked the same LED as the current mid-window pick on all four tested sessions (ch2/ch1/ch0/ch2). No measured change.
Verdict
Skip. The current pick is already robust on this corpus. At best a free tie-breaker; no fidelity gain to justify the added machinery.

③ Ambient-light regression harmful

Hypothesis
The parsed-but-unused ambient column could be regressed out of each LED to remove ambient/motion common-mode before bandpass.
Result
Destabilized detection. At night the ambient channel is near-constant (σ ≈ 36 on a ~650,700 baseline), so full-record least-squares latched onto spurious correlation with wild coefficients (β −132…+17), exploding correction rate to 31–59% and RMSSD (0621 +105.7 ms).
Verdict
Reject as a default. There is no ambient interference to remove in the dark; removing "nothing" naively wrecks the signal. Only conceivable value is daytime/high-motion, and then only gated on high ambient variance with a slow-component fit — never a blanket regression.

⑤ Adaptive motion-gate calibration harmful (naive)

Hypothesis
Fixed thresholds (120 mg / 40 dps) may be mis-scaled per wrist; calibrate the "still" baseline from the recording's own distribution.
Result
Wrist dynamic-motion during sleep is tiny (ACC dyn p99 ≈ 5 mg on 0614), so the fixed gate barely fires (0.33–3.5% flagged) — crude but safe. Naive percentile (p95) normalization was catastrophic: it flagged 99.99% of the still 0614 night as motion, because rescaling turns quiescent micro-noise into "full motion".
Verdict
Do not adopt as prototyped. A safe version would need an absolute mg/dps floor and could only widen, never narrow, the still reference. Low priority; the fixed gate's near-silence on sleep is arguably correct behavior.

5 · Recommendation — propose the winner, don't fuse

Adopt multi-LED waveform fusion alone, as a hybrid that keeps everything already good:

  • Keep consensusBeats for beat gating and the 3/3·2/3·1/3 agreement ribbon (the optical bSQI) — unchanged.
  • Derive foot timing for kept beats from the mean of the orientation-corrected LED waveforms instead of the reference LED alone.
  • Reuse that fused waveform as the morphology / perfusion-index reference to capture the +35–48% SNR.
  • Ship it as an opt-in flag, default-off so the equivalence/regression gates stay byte-identical until validated; re-record fixtures when enabled.

Stacking the others is counterproductive: ③ and ⑤ inject instability, ④ adds machinery for no measured gain. The disciplined outcome of prototyping is a smaller change than the original five-lever wishlist — one clean win instead of a risky stack.

6 · Open items before "ship it"

  • Ground truth (decisive): compare fused-PPG RMSSD to the contemporaneous Polar H10 ECG RR for the same nights. Lower RMSSD is only better if it is closer to the ECG truth, not merely smoother — the clean-data inertness, higher beat yield and lower correction all argue it is real denoising, but the ECG leg decides it.
  • Broader corpus: fold in the remaining big overnights (0610, 0615, 0618, 0620, 0624–0630, 0705) once split, to see how common the large-margin (~9%) sessions are.
  • Second device: everything here is one Verity unit; confirm the SNR-gain and session-dependence hold on other hardware.
  • ⑤ redesign (optional): an absolute-floored, widen-only adaptive gate, if a genuinely mis-scaled wrist ever appears.

7 · Reproducibility notes

All prototypes ran the real ppgdex-dsp.js against sliced real captures; no synthetic data. Baselines are production consensusBeats output. Any adopted change must pass Dex-Test-Suite.html (incl. the PPG env.equiv leg) and, after re-bundling, verify-provenance.html, with fixtures regenerated by re-running the app on committed inputs per the project's re-bundle checklist. New behavior stays behind an opt-in flag with back-compatible signatures (new return fields, not changed ones).
