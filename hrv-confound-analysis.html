Age confounds apnea-driven HRV suppression

A low nocturnal rMSSD is ambiguous — age and apnea both suppress it. Rather than slice into fragile age-stratified subgroups, we fit the whole cohort with an age×AHI interaction model: one coefficient tests on the full N whether apnea’s HRV cost changes with age, and a single age-adjustment recovers the screen.

Simulation — read before citing. The coupling magnitudes below are generator inputs (literature defaults: HRV declines ≈ age, apnea suppresses vagal tone) and are planted additively — interaction = 0. So the fitted age×AHI term should return ≈ null: the interaction test’s job is to detect departure from additivity on the full cohort — the de-confounding question an age-stratified subgroup analysis would otherwise need (and be underpowered) to answer. The contribution is the screening error these magnitudes imply and the single age-adjustment that recovers it — not a clinical validation. Measured rMSSD comes from the real PulseDex DSP.
