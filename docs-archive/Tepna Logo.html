Tepna · brand mark

The T that beats.

One mark, one idea: a T whose crossbar is a single heartbeat — tep (pulse) in tepna (artery). It reads as health-tech without a heart cliché, carries the brand letter, and survives down to a 16 px favicon. Built on the existing teal→blue→purple system.

The mark

T-beat monogramprimary glyph

Why this mark

Selected and refined from the five-direction exploration. The others were either too literal (a bare beat), too soft (radiating rings) or too intricate to hold at small sizes (the branching vessel).

  • Carries the letter. The glyph is unmistakably a “T” — the wordmark and icon reinforce each other.
  • Encodes the name. The crossbar is a QRS heartbeat — tep, the pulse at the root of tepna.
  • Scales. Two strokes, generous caps — clean from a 150 px hero to a 16 px favicon.
  • Fits the suite. Same teal→blue→purple gradient already used across the Dex apps.
