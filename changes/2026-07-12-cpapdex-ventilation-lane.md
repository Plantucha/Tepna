Put CPAPDex's ventilation, flow-limitation and snore metrics on the ganglior bus — the DSP had been computing them per session and then dropping them, so no CPAP ventilation variable had ever reached the Integrator.

`buildSessionFromEdf` computes 32 metrics; `nightMetrics()` aggregated only 15 of them, and the
node-export faithfully emits whatever `nightMetrics()` produces (`metrics: night.metrics`). The other
17 were therefore computed, used for the per-session UI, and discarded — **15 of them carry real data
on ≥168 of 180 nights** in the reference corpus.

This lands the full lane at night level. `_pool` now carries the ventilation / flow-limitation / snore
mask-on slices alongside pressure and leak, and `nightMetrics()` pools them the same way:

  respRateMedian · respRateRange · tidVolMedian · minVentMedian · minVentStability
  flowLimMean · flowLimitedPct · snorePct · snorePressureCorr
  breathCount · breathRate · ieRatio · eprDelta · maskOnLatency · maxLeak

Thresholds and rounding mirror `buildSessionFromEdf` exactly, so a single-session night reads
**identically** at session and night level — verified 15/15 against a real EDF night. Aggregation is
per-metric-kind, not blanket: `maskOnLatency` is a night-onset fact (first session, not an average);
`eprDelta` is a device setting (median over reporting sessions, not a sample percentile); `breathRate`
is total breaths over total therapy time (not a mean of per-session rates); `ieRatio` is a ratio, so
usage-weighted; `snorePressureCorr` is only emitted when the pooled lanes pair 1:1.

All 15 were ALREADY graded in `CPAP_REGISTRY` — they were rendered per session, just never aggregated
— so no registry or evidence-badge work was needed.

Why it matters: on the reference corpus the dropped lane holds the **strongest predictor of nightly
event burden anywhere in the data** (tidal volume, detrended r ≈ 0.5, t ≈ 8) — while the pressure and
leak fields that *were* exported sit at r ≈ 0.16. The suite was exporting its weak predictors and
dropping its strong one.

Also fixes §F5 — `buildLongitudinal()` returned `crossNight: null` **silently** in every Node realm.
`cpapdex-cross.js` was a browser-only IIFE (`})(window)`) exposing nothing to CommonJS, so
`require()`ing it *threw* and the caller swallowed it into a null. It is now dual-realm like its
siblings (`module.exports` + `globalThis` fallback + a side-effect require of the already-dual-realm
`kernel-constants.js` for its `DexKernel` reads), and `cpapdex-dsp.js` resolves it browser-global-first,
CommonJS-second — mirroring how it already resolves the EDF reader. The DSP self-test's unguarded
`root.CPAPCross` read (which threw outright under CommonJS, so `node cpapdex-dsp.js --selftest` had
never once completed) is now an unconditional assertion: **49 passed, 0 failed**, headless.

Export shape moved, so all four CPAPDex fixtures were regenerated by re-running the producing chain
(never hand-edited); both `recording.contentId`s came back unchanged, confirming no structural drift.
