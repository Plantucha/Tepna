Stop counting long-gap interpolation as measured glucose, and detect the vendor clip floor the real Abbott Lingo export actually rails at — TIR read 11 % where the truth was 0 %, and 37 clip artifacts shipped as real nocturnal hypoglycemia.

**§5 — long-gap fill is not measurement.** Short and long gaps both carried `FLAG.GAP`, and
`analyzableIndex` filtered neither — despite a docstring claiming long gaps were "excluded from coverage".
A routine 14 h sensor-change gap was interpolated as a straight line straight into TIR / GMI / CV / MAGE,
the validated-tier headline KPIs. Long gaps now carry `FLAG.GAP_LONG` and are excluded from every
distribution metric (they are still drawn on the chart and still counted as inactive time, so `pctActive`
and `gapMin` keep their meaning). Short gaps remain analyzable — a single missed reading is a sound
physiologic bridge. The same exclusion is applied to the daily distribution and to `nightCV`; and because
an overnight dropout now correctly leaves `nightCV` null, `nocturnal_glucose_risk` no longer silently
substitutes the **daytime** CV — with no nocturnal data the risk is null and the event is not emitted.

**§6 — the clip floor.** `detectClampSaturation` compared a ±1 mg/dL (≈2-wide) bound window against a
5-wide inner slab. Real glucose density *rises* away from the floor, so the slab was almost always the
thicker of the two and the test could essentially never fire. The committed Abbott Lingo export rails at
**54 mg/dL** — 46 readings at the floor against 15 and 14 in the bins beside it, and none below — yet went
undetected, shipping **37 unflagged `nocturnal_hypo` events at conf 0.97** while TBR/LBGI silently
under-counted. The test now compares like for like: the count AT the bound against the mean per-bin count
of the two bins just inside it. Threshold 2.5×, chosen from measured anchors — a genuine nocturnal nadir
piles up only mildly (**1.7×**, an arcsine/turning-point effect), the real Lingo rail is **3.2×**, a hard
synthetic rail is **184×**. Crucially this must NOT over-fire: flagging a real nadir as an artifact would
**hide true hypoglycemia**, which is worse than the bug being fixed, so the false-positive control is
gated alongside the detection.

Fixture regenerated by re-running `compute()` on the committed input (never hand-edited): the export now
carries `clamp:{detected:true, floor:54, blindMetrics:[…]}`, 37 events gain `meta.clampFloor`, and
`sd`/`cv`/`mage`/`tir` shift slightly now that long-gap fill no longer counts. A long-standing assertion
that this fixture was a "clean, unclamped file" was itself wrong and now encodes the truth.
